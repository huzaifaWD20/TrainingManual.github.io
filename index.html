Unleashing the Power of Karakuri

A Journey Through Time and Innovation

What is Karakuri History ?

Karakuri is written in Japanese most commonly as からくり, but sometimes also as 絡繰り, 絡繰, 機巧, or 機関. It originated with mechanical dolls in Japan, called karakuri ningyo (からくり人形). These dolls are first mentioned around 1500 years ago, but were most popular around 200 years ago

Uses of Karakuri

Karakuri can perform a wide range of tasks, from sorting and transporting goods to assembling products and improve productivity.

Karakuri Theory

After an introduction to the topic in earlier pages, I would like to show you some fundamental techniques for karakuri.
The simplicity of Karakuri devices is achieved through the use of basic mechanical principles such as levers, springs, and gears. These simple machines can be combined in various ways to create complex movements and actions

Simple Machines

Lever

A lever is a simple machine made of a rigid beam and a fulcrum. The effort (input force) and load (output force) are applied to either end of the beam. The fulcrum is the point on which the beam pivots. When an effort is applied to one end of the lever, a load is applied at the other end of the lever. This will move a mass upward.

Q) Imagine you have a lever where you apply a force of 200 Newtons to lift and the lever's input force is 100. To find the mechanical advantage, you would divide the output force by the input force ?

  1. 4
  2. 5
  3. 2

Torque - What is Torque ?

Torque refers to the twisting force that causes motion. It also refers to the turning effect. Furthermore, the point of rotation of the object is called the axis of rotation. Individuals make use of this force without realizing this fact.

Pulley - What is a Pulley ?

A simple machine that is used to lift objects. The block and tackle system is a modified form of a pulley. Pulleys are usually used in sets designed to make the amount of force needed to lift something. A pulley may be simple, but this is used in complex things like elevators! Pulleys are also useful in Karakuri.

Springs - What is a Spring ?

Spring balance is a weighing scale. It comprises a spring fixed at one edge with a hook to join an object at the other. It operates by Hooke's law. F = k*x
*The higher the stiffness, the more load can be applied

Q) A spring is displaced by 5 cm and held in place with a force of 500 N. What is the spring constant of the spring?

  1. 500 N = - k x 0.06 m
  2. 500 N = - k x 0.05 m

When a load is applied, the springs are stretched, and the movement of the crossbar with the rack attached rotates the pinion and the load-indicating pointer. The dial is graduated in scale units that depend on the stiffness of the springs: the stiffer springs have larger scale units and higher load capacity

What is Gravitational Force ?

Gravity is the force that pulls objects towards each other and pulls everything towards the center of the Earth. It is an invisible force.
Just as we can analyze the horizontal and vertical components of the motion of an object separately, we can look at components parallel to, and perpendicular to, the sloping surface as well. The normal force of an object placed on a sloping surface is always perpendicular to the surface and the other forces are parallel to the surface.

Gears - What is Gears ?

Gears are mechanical parts with cut teeth designed to fit with teeth on another part so as to transmit or receive force and motion. Gears are also sometimes called toothed wheels or cogged wheels or cogs. The cut teeth are also sometimes called cogs.
*Can we add more gear to make our work easier?

Pulley Weight & Balance
Component Factor Safety Risk Important Factors to consider before installing Karakuri Mechanism
1. Pulley 1. Pulley breaks or fall out of place. 1. Consider the pulley material that is subjected to force and the pulley to be suitable for the weight used.
2. Force & counterweight to reduce weight 2. The weight lifted is higher than the lift height, the bend point is dropped. 2. Consider the weight pulled or lifted by the user is fairly light and the lifting position is suitable.

4. Piece of work 4. Broken Rope. 4. If the weight of the element is > 2kg, then use metal rope instead of normal rope.

Component Factor Safety Risk Important Factors to consider before installing Karakuri Mechanism

Optimized Labor and Streamlined Processes

Flip-Flop Shooter (Hand)

  • Linear Operation with a manual control.
  • Take the work out from the container and return the empty box. Manually tilt and return the table to supply or discharge the container.
  • Labor-saving of returning the work with a small volume.

Flip-Flop Shooter (Foot)

  • Linear Operation with a semi automated control.
  • Take the work out from the container and return the empty box. Foot press and return the table to supply or discharge the container.
  • Labor-saving of returning the work with a small volume.

Flip-Flop Shooter Unit (Tilt)

  • Linear operation along both X and Y axis.
  • This empty box return shooter was designed to have the table stand and stop itself from the weight of the container and work.
  • No need to adjust the elevation rail.

Elevator Unit (Weight)

  • Linear operation along both X and Y axis.
  • When the container filled with work, the unit will descent horizontally to discharge, and after the table return to its origin position, next container will be automatically supplied.
  • No need to adjust the elevation rail.

Elevator Unit (Balancer)

  • Linear operation along both X and Y axis.
  • When the container filled with work, the unit will descent horizontally to discharge, and after the table return to its origin position, next container will be automatically supplied.
